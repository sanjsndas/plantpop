Gardening & Irrigation Tips From Our Experts

Timing is Everything: Water Your Plants Smartly

Water plants early in the morning or late in the evening to maximize absorption and reduce evaporation. Avoid watering during peak sun hours.

Use Mulch to Retain Moisture

Applying organic mulch helps the soil retain moisture, reduce weeds, and regulate temperature, complementing your irrigation system’s efficiency.

Soil Health Matters

Regularly check and amend soil with compost or nutrients to promote healthy root systems that utilize water optimally.

Choose the Right Plants for Your Climate

Native or drought-tolerant plants require less water and maintenance, making them ideal for sustainable gardening with efficient irrigation.

Regular System Check-ups

Schedule periodic maintenance of your irrigation to spot leaks, clogged emitters, or misaligned sprinklers that waste water.

Embrace Smart Technology

Smart controllers and sensors adapt watering schedules based on weather forecasts and soil moisture, saving water and time.

Monitor Water Usage

Keep an eye on monthly water bills to understand your consumption patterns and adjust irrigation accordingly.
